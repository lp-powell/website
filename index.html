ABOUT ME.... Hello, I'm Lucas Powell. I'm a mechanical engineer at Case Western Reserve University. I am a member of my university's SAE Baja team, working on the drivetrain, frame and test engineering team. For the team, I have worked on u-joints, frame weld fixturing, FEA validation, and system test design. I have also worked at BD medical as an engineering intern, working on manufacturing line improvements and participating in internal kaizen events. Another previous job was with Tylok International, where I both worked on the manufacturing floor, and in the design and research department. More information about all the projects mentioned can be found on the projects page. I have manufacturing experience machining with a lathe, mill, CNC router, and Waterjet as well as rapid prototyping with 3d printing, casting and injection molding. On the software side I can use SolidWorks, Siemens NX, Fusion 360, LS-Dyna and Ansys mechanical. I also have some experience with micro-controllers, control circuits and programming languages like C, MATLAB and python.

I am interested in test engineering, alternative power and motorsports. At Tylok I designed a high-pressure test system, and this was some of the best engineering work I have done and sparked an interest in pushing designs to their limit. I want to work in alternative energy, as it is the future of power generation and technologies like the fusion reactor is the cutting edge of technology and because of this, I have started my own fusion project. Motorsports is another passion of mine, both as a spectator, and hopefully a competitor.
